# Analysis Framework

## Long-Term Framework

Use this when the question is "Is this a business we want to own or accumulate over time?"

Evaluate across these dimensions:

1. Growth quality
- Revenue growth durability
- Organic versus acquisition-driven growth
- Guidance trajectory
- Estimate revision direction

2. Profitability quality
- Gross margin trend
- Operating leverage
- Free-cash-flow conversion
- Return profile if available

3. Business strength
- Category position
- Customer adoption durability
- Product cycle or platform tailwinds
- Evidence of pricing power or switching costs

4. Management and execution
- Beat/raise consistency
- Clarity and credibility of guidance
- Willingness to acknowledge weakness
- Capital allocation discipline

5. Market support
- Relative strength versus sector and market
- Post-earnings reaction quality
- Institutional sponsorship proxies if available

6. Valuation context
- Multiple versus growth and margin profile
- Whether good fundamentals are already fully priced
- Whether multiple expansion is likely or unlikely from here

## Short-Term Setup Framework

Use this when the question is "Is there a high-quality tactical opportunity over the next days or weeks?"

Evaluate across these dimensions:

1. Trend structure
- Price above or below key moving averages
- Higher highs / higher lows or breakdown structure
- Distance from recent breakout or support zone

2. Relative strength
- Strength versus benchmark and sector
- Strength before and after catalysts
- Whether strength is broad-based or one-day noise

3. Participation
- Volume expansion on advances
- Muted volume on pullbacks
- Accumulation/distribution clues

4. Compression and expansion
- Volatility contraction
- Range tightening before catalyst
- Expansion confirmation after breakout

5. Catalyst path
- Earnings date proximity
- Analyst revision cluster
- Product, regulatory, macro, or sector event

6. Timing and risk
- Crowded entry after an extended move
- Binary event exposure
- Nearby invalidation level
- Market regime fit

## Risk Lens

Apply this to both horizons:

- Thesis risk: what would make the core idea wrong?
- Timing risk: what could be correct eventually but wrong now?
- Data risk: which signal is noisy or unavailable?
- Regime risk: which signals behave differently in this market environment?

## Ranking Design Guidance

When building system logic:

- Use component scores first, then compose them
- Keep long-term and short-term composite scores separate
- Make each score explainable in plain English
- Keep room for "insufficient evidence" rather than forcing every stock into a strong view
- Prefer stable 1-5 or 1-10 bands unless there is strong reason for finer granularity
