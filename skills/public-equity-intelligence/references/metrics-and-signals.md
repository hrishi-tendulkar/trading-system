# Metrics And Signals

## Core company metrics

- Revenue growth: prefer year-over-year plus sequential context when seasonality matters
- Gross margin: useful for pricing power, mix shift, or execution quality
- Operating margin: useful for leverage and discipline, but normalize for one-time items
- Free cash flow: useful, but be careful with timing distortions and working-capital swings
- EPS growth: useful as a market signal, but often less robust than revenue plus margin
- Guidance change: one of the most important practical signals for both horizons
- Estimate revision: critical for momentum in expectations, not just results

## Core market metrics

- Relative strength: compare against both the benchmark and the relevant sector/industry group
- Volume ratio: current volume versus average volume; use as confirmation rather than a standalone signal
- Moving-average structure: useful as a regime and trend filter, not a thesis by itself
- Volatility compression: identify range tightening or ATR contraction before expansion
- Gap behavior: distinguish constructive breakaway gaps from exhaustion gaps

## Event-driven signals

- Earnings proximity: treat pre-event and post-event logic differently
- Post-earnings reaction: often more informative than the headline numbers alone
- Analyst actions: care more about clustered revisions than isolated headlines
- News classification: separate company-specific, sector-specific, macro, and low-signal news
- Filing changes: focus on guidance language, risk-factor changes, capital allocation, and unusual disclosures

## Useful implementation proxies

If a desired concept is abstract, map it to a measurable proxy:

- Narrative strength -> estimate revisions, guidance trajectory, call tone shifts, recurring theme frequency
- Institutional interest -> liquidity, sustained relative strength, ownership trends if available
- Competitive positioning -> margin durability, win-rate commentary, market-share clues, pricing commentary
- Momentum quality -> relative strength plus constructive pullback behavior plus volume pattern

## Common pitfalls

- Using trailing valuation multiples without checking forward expectations
- Treating all revenue growth as equal regardless of margin or durability
- Ignoring sector context when comparing stocks
- Treating one strong candle as evidence of a setup
- Confusing earnings volatility with edge
- Building signals that depend on data sources you do not actually have

## Minimum evidence standard

Before adding a metric or signal to the system, define:

1. What it measures
2. Why it matters
3. How it is calculated
4. What data source will supply it
5. What failure modes or distortions apply
6. How it should be stored and versioned
