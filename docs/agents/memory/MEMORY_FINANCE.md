# MEMORY_FINANCE

## Durable Notes

- [2026-05-22] The finance edge comes from disciplined separation of horizons and signal families, not from one grand composite score.
- [2026-05-22] For weekly swing recommendations, explicit setup families with entry-quality filters are more trustworthy than generic strength scores; the system must be able to say "good stock, wrong entry" without ambiguity.
- [2026-05-23] With only daily OHLCV and limited point-in-time event history, short-term edge should come from regime filtering, relative-strength persistence, entry-quality discipline, and setup-specific expectancy rather than from broader narrative or unsupported revision signals.
- [2026-05-23] Broader `S&P 100`-class replay over ~5 years showed baseline `Buy now` logic was too loose; regime gating was the highest-leverage fast improvement, and selectivity improved portfolio-level results more than adding extra narrative complexity.
- [2026-05-25] Canonical strategy-separated replay validated unequal trust across sleeves: `Breakout Confirmation` earned promotion now, `Sector-Confirmed Pullback Continuation` only earned a narrowed mainline role, `ETF Trend / Rotation` remained canonical but not promoted under its current daily-entry definition, and `Selective Mean Reversion` stayed research only.
- [2026-06-02] Strategy 1 refinement narrowed `Breakout Confirmation` promotion to triggered breakouts in `Risk-on` or `Selective risk-on` regimes with confirmed sector context. This variant cleared the promotion bar after `20 bps` friction with `19.94%` net annualized return, `+5.93%` annualized excess versus `SPY` buy-and-hold, `+18.50%` versus exposure-aware `SPY`, and `-18.10%` max drawdown versus `SPY` at `-25.36%`.
- [2026-06-02] The broad current breakout baseline should remain research context, not sufficient board logic: it beat exposure-aware `SPY` but missed `SPY` buy-and-hold annualized excess and had worse drawdown. Stricter relative-strength and entry-quality filters also stayed research-only because they reduced the edge.
- [2026-06-07] `breakout-confirmation.v2` is now the production Strategy 1 rule used by weekly generation: only triggered breakouts in supportive regimes with confirmed sector context become `Buy now`; near-trigger setups remain `Wait for confirmation`; broad unsupported breakouts are not promoted.
- [2026-06-06] Expanding toward `S&P 500` coverage should improve candidate discovery and replay sample size, but it must not lower the promotion bar for weekly action names or imply qualitative conviction for every constituent.
- [2026-06-06] The first S&P 100 weekly run produced a sparse action distribution: `2` Buy now, `4` Wait for pullback, `8` Wait for confirmation, `18` Hold, `76` No action, and `1` Benchmark reference. This supports the broad-scan / sparse-board product posture.
- [2026-06-07] Public Equity Investing plugin should first be used in two concrete strategy-support experiments: `Buy now` veto checks for action-board names and post-earnings continuation research packets for technically strong post-print names. Plugin output must become structured fields such as `event_veto`, `thesis_damage`, `decision_effect`, `quality_of_print`, and `next_falsifier`; it should not upgrade names to `Buy now` or bypass promoted-sleeve rules.
- [2026-06-07] Selective Mean Reversion was re-tested across defensive and stress-only rebound variants with daily `OHLCV`, `20 bps` friction, weekly top-`5` portfolios, `5D` / `10D` / `15D` holding windows, `SPY` buy-and-hold, and exposure-aware `SPY`. The best `stress_v1` `15D` result beat exposure-aware `SPY` by `+6.10%` annualized but lagged `SPY` buy-and-hold by `-7.22%` annualized and had `43.04%` of signals in one year, so `selective-mean-reversion.v1` remains research-only with no sandbox, paper-live, or fresh-capital board eligibility.
- [2026-06-07] Ranked weekly `ETF Trend / Rotation` remains research-only. The best tested variant, top `2` sector ETFs in supportive regimes with strict `20D`/`60D` RS and trend gates, returned `5.99%` net annualized after `20 bps` friction and beat exposure-aware `SPY` by `4.13%`, but lagged `SPY` buy-and-hold by `8.28%` annualized. ETF rotation can stay as an exposure appendix or weak-single-name substitute context, but it should not feed the main board as promoted `Buy now` logic.
- [2026-06-07] `Sector-Confirmed Pullback Continuation` was re-tested for a possible v2 across pullback depth, extension band, sector confirmation, regime, RS consistency, ATR burden, support proximity, and watch-only extended-strength separation. No narrowed live-entry variant cleared the full promotion bar: strict support-proximity improved exposure-aware information quality to `0.232` and beat exposure-aware `SPY` by `+11.08%` annualized, but lagged `SPY` buy-and-hold by `-0.13%`; current v1 remained best on absolute return at `18.82%` net annualized and `+4.54%` versus `SPY` buy-and-hold. Keep `sector-confirmed-pullback-continuation.v1` active; do not promote broad dip-buying, deep pullbacks, or extended watch names as live `Buy on pullback`.
