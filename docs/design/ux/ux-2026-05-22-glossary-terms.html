Glossary

Terms used on the page

The production product should never force you to guess what a label means. Every important term on the weekly overview can link here.

Glossary

Selective Risk-On

Definition: A market posture where the broad tape is constructive enough to take risk, but not strong enough to buy every apparent breakout without extra discipline.

Why it matters here: This affects how aggressive the weekly action list should be. In a selective risk-on tape, patience and entry quality matter more than volume of trades.

Glossary

Refined Score

Definition: A compact ranking helper built from trend, momentum, relative strength, proximity to highs, and penalties for excess volatility or overextension.

Why it matters here: It is a supporting ranking signal, not a standalone recommendation. A higher score helps a stock surface, but the final call should still come from the decision basis and entry-quality checks.

Glossary

Decision Basis

Definition: The named logic behind the recommendation. In this prototype, a decision basis can be a setup family, a risk guardrail, or a context lens.

Why it matters here: This prevents us from calling everything a 'strategy' when some rows are really just contextual or risk-management rules.

Glossary

Trade Setup

Definition: A repeatable short-term trading pattern with entry rules, invalidation logic, time horizon, and a path to historical replay.

Why it matters here: This is closer to standard trading language than an invented category label. Examples include constructive pullback continuation or breakout confirmation.

Glossary

Risk Rule

Definition: A rule that suppresses or modifies action even if a chart looks acceptable.

Why it matters here: The clearest example in this MLP is avoiding fresh entries right before earnings. This is not alpha generation by itself; it is damage control.

Glossary

Context Lens

Definition: A row or rule used to interpret the market rather than directly recommend a stock purchase.

Why it matters here: The SPY row is a context lens. It helps explain posture and relative strength, but it is not competing with every other stock idea.

Glossary

Relative Strength vs SPY

Definition: The stock’s 20-day return minus SPY’s 20-day return.

Why it matters here: This tells us whether the stock is merely going up with the market or actually acting stronger than the benchmark.

Glossary

Event Risk

Definition: The risk that a scheduled catalyst, most often earnings, dominates the next move and overwhelms the chart setup.

Why it matters here: A decent chart can still be a weak weekly entry if a major event is only a few days away.

Glossary

Support

Definition: A price area where the stock has recently found demand or where a key moving average offers structural support.

Why it matters here: Support helps anchor entry zones and invalidation levels.

Glossary

Resistance

Definition: A price area where the stock previously struggled to move higher or where a breakout trigger sits.

Why it matters here: Resistance matters because some setups need confirmation through that level before they deserve capital.

Glossary

Invalidation

Definition: The price condition that tells us the current setup no longer holds.

Why it matters here: This is more useful than a vague stop concept because it ties risk management directly to why the trade exists.

Glossary

ATR

Definition: Average True Range, a volatility proxy based on recent trading ranges and gaps.

Why it matters here: ATR helps size targets and buffers so the model does not pretend every stock moves the same way.
