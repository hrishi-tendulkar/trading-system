What We Challenged

Adversarial refinement

Challenge 1

Pushback: This could just be disguised momentum chasing.

Refinement: Add an overextension filter so names too far above the 20-day average do not qualify as immediate buys.

Challenge 2

Pushback: A pullback can look constructive right before a deeper breakdown.

Refinement: Require price to remain above the 20-day and 50-day averages so the pattern stays trend-following rather than bottom-fishing.

Challenge 3

Pushback: A strong chart before earnings can still be a coin flip next week.

Refinement: Let the event-risk guardrail override the setup when earnings are too close.

Challenge 4

Pushback: Even good entries can fail in a sloppy market.

Refinement: Condition the setup on market posture so selective tapes demand more patience than broad risk-on tapes.

Current Rule Spine

What survives after that debate

  • Supportive or at least selective risk-on market posture
  • Price above the 20-day and 50-day averages
  • Positive relative strength versus SPY, preferably across more than one lookback window
  • Only modest extension above the 20-day average so the entry is still usable
  • Defined support, practical invalidation, and no near-term earnings override

How to Read the Replay

Backtest interpretation

The replay is a trust-calibration tool, not a seal of approval. If the basis has poor historical outcomes, that should meaningfully soften the language of the live recommendation even when the current chart looks attractive.

This page exists so you can challenge both the setup logic and the setup evidence before acting on real money. The correct output is sometimes "logical pattern, but only moderate trust."
