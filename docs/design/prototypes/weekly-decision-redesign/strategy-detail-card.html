Plain-English purpose

Stops you from buying too early.

The point is simple: do not buy a stock just because it looks strong. Wait until price clears a level that proves buyers are still in control.

Simple action rules
  • Buy: buy only after the stock clears the required price level.
  • Wait: keep it on alert because the entry is not clean yet.
  • Reject: do not buy because the setup is too weak, too late, or too risky.
  • Valid until: the signal expires at this week’s close unless it triggers first.
  • Expected hold: usually 5-15 trading days after a valid entry.

When this helps

Best use case

  • The market is healthy enough to buy selected winners.
  • The stock’s sector is also acting well.
  • The stock is close to the buy level, not already far above it.
When this hurts

Common mistakes

  • Buying after the move already happened.
  • Buying when the broader market is getting weaker.
  • Trusting one strong day when follow-through disappears.
Before a buy

Minimum proof

  • A clear buy price.
  • A clear sell price if the trade fails.
  • Enough upside left to make the risk worth taking.

Limits

What this strategy cannot tell you

  • This is not a business-quality strategy. It is about short-term price confirmation.
  • It should not override event risk, earnings freezes, or broken market posture.
  • It should never produce a buy without an entry, target, and sell level.
